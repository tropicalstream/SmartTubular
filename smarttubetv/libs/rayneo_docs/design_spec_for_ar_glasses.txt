1. Core Design Principles (Core Principles)
The core of AI/AR glasses design lies in  "Augment without Distraction" .
- Real World First : UI is an "annotation" of the real world rather than a "mask".  Black (#000000) is transparent , and the transparent area must be maximized to ensure users' safety perception of the environment.
- Heads-up & Glanceable : Information should be presented within the natural line of sight, and interactions must support blind operation. Avoid users having to look down or focus on reading for an extended period.
- Minimalism: Limited by FOV (Field of View) and computing power, all decorative elements are removed. Only the core text and icons are retained, pursuing extremely low information density.
- Audio-Visual Synergy: Visuals only carry key "road sign" information, while detailed descriptions and secondary feedback are delegated to voice/audio interaction.

3. Layout & Zoning
3.1 Degree of Freedom Mode (DoF Mode)
0 DOF (Recommended)
- Description
AR glasses do not track any movement of the user's head; virtual information is fixedly displayed in front of the wearer's eyes and moves with the wearer's movement.
- Applicable Scenarios
Currently, all system-level applications of the X Series glasses adopt the 0 DoF mode.
- Interaction Limitation
Users cannot change their perspective by turning their heads or moving, and virtual information always remains in a fixed position.
- Suitable for Interaction
Touchpad or button interaction, voice interaction.
[Image]

3 DOF (used in combination with scenarios)
- Description
AR glasses track the rotation of the user's head along the x, y, and z axes, but do not track positional translation.
- Applicable Scenarios
360° panoramic video or panoramic image browsing.
- Interaction Restrictions
Users can only move their perspective by "turning their heads" and cannot "walk into" virtual content. A "self-righting mechanism" is required.
- Suitable for interaction
Touchpad or button interaction, voice interaction, and gesture interaction with fixed depth.
[Image]

6 DOF (Exploration Scene)
- Description
AR glasses can simultaneously track the rotation and positional translation of the user's head along the x-axis, y-axis, and z-axis, allowing the user to move freely within three-dimensional space.
- Applicable Scenarios
Virtual object placement and real-time interaction, such as furniture preview, industrial assembly, and virtual navigation.
- Interaction Limitations
Requires support from cameras and SLAM algorithms to avoid coordinate drift; when virtual objects interact with the real environment, issues such as occlusion, lighting, and spatial position relationships need to be considered.
- Suitable for interaction
Gesture interaction with spatial depth, 6 DoF controllers, eye tracking interaction, etc.
PS: High power consumption, it is recommended to use only for short-term exploration scenarios
[Image]

3.2 Functional Zoning (Zoning)
- Top (Top): Temporary Notification Area (Toast), displays status messages such as "Connected" and "Saved successfully", and automatically disappears after 2-3 seconds.
- Center: Core interaction area, displays AI responses, incoming call cards, and core operation objects.
- Bottom (Bottom): Navigation and Guidance Area, displays Bottom Bar (3-4 icons) or operation prompts (e.g., "Double-click to exit").
- Left (Left): Media feedback area, corresponding to the physical interaction of the left temple (volume/brightness adjustment feedback).

4. Visual Design System (VDS)
4.1 Color Strategy
- Background Color :  Force Pure Black (#000000) . Black Non-Luminous = Transparent.  Prohibited  to Use White or Bright Full-Screen Background (Causes Blindness + High Power Consumption).
- Theme Color : High-saturation, high-brightness cool tones must be used to ensure visibility under strong outdoor light.
  - ✅ Recommended: Cyan (#4DE2FF), Fluorescent Green (#44FF66), Purple (#8566FF).
  - ⚠️ Use with caution: Red (only used for deletion/extremely high-risk warnings, prone to causing fatigue).
  - ❌ Disabled: Gradient colors (prone to color banding; if necessary, prioritize the blue channel), dark gray (invisible outdoors).
4.2 Typography Specification (Typography)
- Minimum font size: 16px, less than 16px is unreadable on AR devices.
- Font Weight: Recommended Bold or Medium. Regular/Light fonts are prone to being confused with environmental textures in a perspective background.
- Level:
  - Title : 28px - 40px (Bold)
  - 正文: 20px - 24px (Regular/Medium)
  - Auxiliary : 16px - 18px
- Layout: Suggest 1.2 line spacing. Text should be streamlined into short sentences or keyword lists, avoiding long paragraphs.
4.3 3D and Graphics
- 3D creatives
  - Only applicable to Low Poly, with the number of faces per model < 5000.
  - It is recommended to use solid colors for materials, and exercise caution when using warm colors over large areas.
  - The model uses PBR materials first, with the recommended maximum size of PBR textures being 512x512px. Limiting the size helps reduce caching.
  - It is not recommended to use particle effects and physical systems, as a large amount of computation will increase CPU power consumption, leading to overheating and frame drops, resulting in a laggy screen.
  - Skybox/HDR Environment: There is no need to render the environment texture. If you add a sky environment, please set the camera background to black to ensure that the Skybox is not rendered.
- Icons: Prioritize using linear icons (Outline) or high-contrast flat icons, and avoid complex details.

5.2 Voice & MultiModal Machine Learning (Voice & AI)
- First Priority: Input the preferred voice.
- Feedback: AI responses follow the principle of "short sentences + waveform animation effects". Avoid displaying large blocks of text on the screen, and present detailed content through TTS playback.

- Watch (X3 Pro): Apple Watch gesture interaction (pinch to confirm, twist wrist to select), suitable for minimalist operation.

7. Performance & Power Red Line (Performance & Power)
Due to the high integration of the glasses, the overall heat dissipation capacity is relatively weak. Therefore, it is necessary to control power consumption to prevent laggy and frequency reduction issues caused by excessive heating.
- Impact of UI layer design on power consumption
  - Minimize highlight animations or continuous dynamic content
  - Use transparent backgrounds reasonably to avoid overloading the GPU
  - Optimize layer composition and resource formats (SVG is superior to PNG/GIF)
  - In terms of color combination, use the green channel more and the red channel less (red light has the highest power consumption)
- APL (Average Picture Level):
  - Continuously display interface APL < 13%.
  - Instantaneous Peak APL < 25%.
  - Principle: The darker the screen, the more power is saved, and the fewer pixels are lit, the better.
- Refresh Rate: UI is recommended to be controlled at 30fps, and non-game applications do not require 60fps.
- Logical Threads: Keep them within 4 to prevent CPU overheating and frequency reduction.

8. Acceptance Inspection Checklist
Before delivering design drafts or code, the following checks must be passed:
1. [ ] Is the background pure black (#000000)? (Ensure transparency)
2. [ ] Is the minimum font size > 16px? (Ensure readability)
3. [ ] Is the line width ≥ 2px? (Prevent breakage/aliasing)
4. [ ] Is APL < 13%? (Is there a large area of luminous region?)
5. [ ] Does the walking scene block the  of the center of the field of view? (Ensure walking safety)
6. [ ] Is voice/touch operation guidance provided? (to prevent users from not knowing how to interact)
